Burnley Borough Council paid £2.4M to external consultants in the 2024-25 financial year, according to spending transparency data published by the council.

The figure represents a 34% increase on the £1.79M spent in 2023-24. The largest single recipient was Liberata, the outsourcing firm that manages revenues and benefits services for the council, receiving £890,000 across 12 monthly payments.

## Where the money went

The top five consultant payments by value were:

| Supplier | Amount | Service |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Liberata UK | £890,000 | Revenues & benefits |
| Capita Business Services | £312,000 | IT infrastructure |
| KPMG LLP | £187,000 | Audit services |
| Jacobs Engineering | £156,000 | Planning support |
| WYG Group | £98,000 | Environmental assessments |

Together these five firms accounted for 68% of total consultant spending.

## Context

Burnley is a district council serving 87,600 residents. Its net revenue budget for 2024-25 was £12.8M, meaning consultant spending represented approximately 19% of the total budget.

The council's spokesperson said external expertise was required for "specialist functions that cannot be delivered in-house cost-effectively."

Sources: Burnley Council spending data (FOI ref BC/2025/0341), Statement of Accounts 2024-25.
