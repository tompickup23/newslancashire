Pendle Borough Council's planning committee voted 7-4 to approve a 350-home development on land between Colne and Barrowford.

The decision came despite 1,200 written objections from residents, who raised concerns about traffic congestion on the A56, loss of green belt land, and pressure on local schools and GP surgeries.

The developer, Taylor Wimpey, committed to providing 30% affordable housing and a contribution of £1.2M towards local infrastructure improvements.

Councillor James Henderson, who voted against the application, said the decision "prioritises developer profits over the wishes of the community."

Sources: Pendle Borough Council Planning Committee minutes, 8 April 2026.
