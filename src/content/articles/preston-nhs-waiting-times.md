Average accident and emergency waiting times at Royal Preston Hospital reached 14 hours in March 2026, according to NHS England data. The figure is the worst on record for the hospital and well above the national average of 8.2 hours.

Only 52% of patients were seen within the four-hour target, against the NHS standard of 95%.

Lancashire Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust, which runs Royal Preston, said the pressures were driven by a combination of high demand, delayed discharges, and staffing shortages.

The hospital declared a critical incident twice during March, diverting ambulances to Blackpool Victoria and Chorley and South Ribble hospitals.

Sources: NHS England A&E Attendances and Emergency Admissions, March 2026; Lancashire Teaching Hospitals Trust Board papers.
